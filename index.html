Context: Energy Access Gap

Despite grid connectivity, many rural households in Jharkhand experience unreliable power, voltage fluctuations, and high monthly expenditure on energy substitutes.

Reliability

Extended outages and inconsistent supply impact livelihoods and education.

Affordability

Upfront solar costs remain inaccessible for low-income households.

Infrastructure Gap

Decentralized renewable assets are under-deployed in rural regions.

Our Infrastructure Model

Suryamitr Connect deploys and operates small-scale rooftop solar systems through a community-supported, reward-based funding model.

Households

Access reliable electricity without upfront capital or long-term debt.

Supporters

Provide voluntary financial support and receive non-financial recognition.

Platform

Ensures deployment, monitoring, maintenance, and public reporting.

ESG Alignment

Governance

Governance & Compliance Posture

Suryamitr Connect follows a conservative compliance-first approach:

  • All support is reward-based and voluntary
  • No equity, profit-sharing, or financial return is offered
  • No asset ownership or operational control is transferred
  • Cross-border contributions are processed via regulated payment platforms
  • No carbon credits are issued or sold at this stage

How to Support

Individuals and institutions may support this initiative through transparent, reward-based contributions.
