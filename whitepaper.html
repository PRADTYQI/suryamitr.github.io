Low-Cost Solar Energy Monitoring

Technical Whitepaper documenting a proof of concept for real-time solar energy monitoring using open-source hardware and software.

Executive Summary

This whitepaper presents a technical Proof of Concept (PoC) demonstrating how small-scale solar energy systems can be monitored in real time using low-cost, open-source components. The objective is to validate feasibility, transparency, and scalability for decentralized and rural solar infrastructure.

Background

Distributed solar installations often lack continuous monitoring due to high costs and proprietary systems. This results in limited visibility, delayed fault detection, and reduced accountability.

The PoC described here focuses on an open and minimal architecture that prioritizes accessibility and auditability.

Problem Definition

  • No real-time performance visibility
  • Manual fault identification
  • Limited historical energy data
  • High cost of vendor-locked monitoring solutions

Proposed Solution

The system integrates a current sensor with an ESP32 microcontroller to collect solar output data and transmit it over Wi-Fi to an open-source energy management platform.

System Architecture

Solar Panel
   ↓
Charge Controller
   ↓
Current Sensor (ACS712)
   ↓
ESP32 Microcontroller (Wi-Fi)
   ↓
OpenEMS Platform

Hardware Components

  • ESP32 microcontroller with Wi-Fi
  • ACS712 Hall-effect current sensor
  • Solar charge controller
  • Low-voltage wiring and connectors

Approximate electronics cost (excluding panel): ₹1,100.

Software Stack

  • Firmware: Arduino (ESP32)
  • Data transmission: Wi-Fi (HTTP / MQTT)
  • Energy platform: OpenEMS
  • Visualization: Web-based dashboard

Results

  • Stable real-time power monitoring
  • Daily energy generation tracking
  • Reliable data transmission
  • Feasibility proven at low cost

Scalability & Use Cases

  • Rural electrification projects
  • Community solar installations
  • Schools and health centers
  • CSR and ESG monitoring initiatives

Limitations

  • Single-parameter sensing (current only)
  • Proof-of-concept scale
  • Internet connectivity required

Future Roadmap

  • Voltage and energy billing integration
  • Automated fault alerts
  • Multi-node aggregation
  • Third-party audits and verification

Conclusion

This Proof of Concept demonstrates that transparent, real-time solar monitoring can be achieved using open technologies at minimal cost. The approach supports data-driven decision-making and ESG-aligned infrastructure development.
